BIRTH AND EARLY LIFE

Alan Mathison Turing was born on June 23, 1912, in London. His father, Julius Mathison Turing, was a British member of the Indian Civil Service, a profession that required him to spend much of his time in the subcontinent. Turing's mother, Ethel Sara Stoney, was the daughter of a chief engineer in the Madras and Southern Mahratta Railway. She spent most of her time in England, where she had a relationship with Julius Turing and became pregnant with Alan.

Alan Turing's parents separated when he was just a few months old, and he and his brother, John, were raised by their mother in Hastings, England. Turing's relationship with his mother was close, and she encouraged him to pursue his interests in mathematics and science. She enrolled him in St. Michael's, a day school, and later at the Sherborne School, a boarding school in Dorset.
